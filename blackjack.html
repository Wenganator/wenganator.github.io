Master the mathematically optimal play for every situation and reduce the house edge to under 0.5%.

🎯 The Objective

Beat the dealer by getting closer to 21 without going over. A Blackjack (Ace + 10-value card) pays 3:2 and beats any regular 21. If the dealer busts, all non-busted players win automatically.

🃏 Card Values

  • 2–9 — Face value
  • 10, J, Q, K — All worth 10
  • Ace — Worth 11, counted as 1 only if 11 would cause a bust. A hand with an Ace counting as 11 is a soft hand.
💡 "Soft 18" = Ace + 7 where the Ace counts as 11. It's soft because you can't bust by drawing one card — the Ace simply reverts to 1.

🔄 How a Round Works

  • Place your bet before cards are dealt
  • Deal — You get two face-up cards; dealer gets one face-up (upcard) and one face-down (hole card)
  • Act on your hand before the dealer reveals
  • Dealer reveals hole card and must hit until reaching hard 17+
  • Settle bets — Higher total wins; blackjack pays 3:2; tie is a push

✋ Your Options

H — Hit S — Stand D — Double Down P — Split R — Surrender Ds — Dbl/Stand

Hit (H)

Take another card. Repeat until you stand or bust.

Stand (S)

Keep your current total. Your turn ends.

Double Down (D)

Double your bet and receive exactly one more card. Only available on the initial two cards.

Split (P)

When your two cards share a value, split them into two hands, each with its own bet. Always split Aces and 8s. Never split 5s or 10s.

Surrender (R)

Forfeit your hand and recover half your bet. Only available on the initial two cards. When the chart shows Rh, surrender if offered — otherwise Hit.

Double/Stand (Ds)

Double if allowed (e.g., soft 18 vs 4–6), otherwise Stand. Common for soft hands with good doubling potential.

🏦 Dealer Rules

  • Dealer must hit any total of 16 or less
  • S17 (standard, player-friendly): Dealer stands on all 17s
  • H17 (worse for player): Dealer hits soft 17 — adds ~0.22% to house edge
  • Dealer has no choices — they follow fixed rules with no strategy decisions

🧠 What is Basic Strategy?

Basic Strategy is the mathematically optimal decision for every combination of your hand vs. the dealer's upcard. Computed from millions of simulated hands, it reduces the house edge to roughly 0.4–0.5% — the lowest of any casino table game.

⚡ Basic Strategy doesn't guarantee winning sessions. It minimizes your average loss rate over time by maximizing expected value on every decision.

The Logic Behind Key Plays

  • Hit 12–16 vs dealer 7–A: Dealer is likely to make a strong hand. You'll lose anyway if you stand on 16 vs a dealer 20 — take the chance to improve.
  • Stand 12+ vs dealer 4–6: Dealer has a "bust card" and must hit — let them do the work.
  • Double 10–11 vs weak dealers: High chance of landing 20 or 21, and you earn double on the win.
  • Always split 8s: Hard 16 is the worst hand. Two hands starting at 8 are significantly better EV.
  • Never split 10s: A total of 20 wins ~85% of the time. Splitting it away is a huge mistake.
  • Surrender 15/16 vs dealer 10: You're a heavy underdog — recovering 50¢ beats the expected outcome of playing.

🚫 Common Mistakes

  • Standing on 16 vs dealer 7–A (should hit or surrender)
  • Not doubling 11 vs dealer 2–10 (biggest missed EV mistake)
  • Splitting 10s (never do this — 20 is a powerhouse)
  • Not splitting Aces (always split them)
  • Taking insurance (a sucker bet with ~7.7% expected loss)
  • Standing on soft 17 (A,6) — always hit or double
  • Standing on 12 vs dealer 2–3 out of "caution" (correct: hit)
